I Love Dinosaurs!

Dinosaurs are some of the coolest creatures to ever walk the Earth. Their immense size and the sheer length of time they existed (millions of years!) makes them a fascinating subject to study and learn about. Dinosaurs existed for about 165 to 180 MILLION years and have left many records that show how they lived through fossils. To show just how long that is, we live closer in time to the existence of Tyrannosaurus Rex than it did to Stegosaurus! Dinosaurs have been an enduring part of pop culture, from action figures to block buster movies to art and more.



There are two main types of Dinosaurs: Saurischia, meaning lizard-hipped, and Ornithischia, meaning bird-hipped. As you may have guessed, this has to do with the type of hip bones these different species have. Saurischia includes therapods like Tyrannosaurus Rex and long-necked Saurapods like Brachiosaurus. Ornithischia includes armored tank-like Stegasaurids, horned Ceratopsids like Stegosaurus, and more. Modern day birds are descendents of the therapod group, making them technically dinosaurs even if the original clade did not survive the massive extinction event.

Factors of extinction of the Dinosaurs

  1. The main factor of course is the catastrophic asteroid impact centered in the Chicxulub crater in Mexico
  2. Another contributing factor is volcanic activity that released massive amounts of ash and gas into the atmosphere
    • This would have weakened the ecosystem prior to the asteroid impact
    • Therefore these ecosystems would have been more vulnerable to such an event
  3. Other less supported theories include egg predation by mammals, disease, or physiological limitations
